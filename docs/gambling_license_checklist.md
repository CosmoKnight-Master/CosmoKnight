## Part 1: Determine Your Business Model

### Gambling Business Types in Nevada

**A) Online Sports Betting**
- **Regulator:** Nevada Gaming Control Board (NGCB)
- **License Type:** Retail Sportsbook License (if partnering with casino) or Remote Sports Betting License
- **Requirements:** Strong AML/compliance program, age verification, geolocation
- **Cost (Estimate):** $5,000–$20,000 initial application + annual renewal

**B) Daily Fantasy Sports (DFS)**
- **Regulator:** Nevada Secretary of State or NGCB (regulatory overlap)
- **License Type:** DFS Operator License
- **Requirements:** Player funding safeguards, consumer protection, disclosures
- **Cost (Estimate):** $500–$5,000 initial + annual

**C) Online Casino / Poker**
- **Regulator:** Nevada Gaming Control Board
- **License Type:** Online Gambling License (Class 2 or Class 3, depending on games)
- **Requirements:** Highest compliance bar; background checks on operators; robust RNG certification
- **Cost (Estimate):** $10,000–$50,000+ application + annual

**D) Affiliate / Referral Model**
- **Regulator:** NGCB (affiliate disclosures required)
- **License Type:** Affiliate Program Registration (lighter touch)
- **Requirements:** Revenue sharing disclosure, responsible gambling messaging
- **Cost (Estimate):** $500–$2,000

## Part 2: State-Level Licensing (Nevada)

### Nevada Gaming Control Board (NGCB) — Primary Regulator

**Contact Info:**
- Address: 1150 North Ridgetop Road, Reno, NV 89520
- Phone: (775) 687-6500
- Website: https://gaming.nv.gov/

### Required Applications & Filings

| Item | Type | Timeline | Cost | Deadline |
|------|------|----------|------|----------|
| Notice of Intent to Apply | Pre-Application | 30 days before formal app | Free | Submit to NGCB |
| Gaming License Application | Full Application | 60–180 days review | $5K–$50K | After Notice approved |
| Background Investigation | Security Check | 90+ days | Included in app fee | Required before approval |
| Quarterly Compliance Reports | Ongoing | Due by 30 days after quarter-end | Free | March 31, June 30, Sept 30, Dec 31 |
| Annual Renewal | License Renewal | 60 days | Varies | Before expiration |

### Step-by-Step Process (Outline)

#### 1) Pre-Application Consultation
- Contact NGCB Gaming Licensing Bureau
- Schedule consultation call
- Discuss your business model and compliance plan
- Estimated time: 1–2 weeks

#### 2) File Notice of Intent
- Prepare business plan summary
- Identify ownership structure (members/managers)
- Describe consumer protections (KYC, age verification, geolocation)
- File with NGCB (email preferred)
- Estimated time: 30 days for NGCB feedback

#### 3) Prepare Full Application
**Required Attachments:**
- Detailed business plan (5–20 pages)
- Organizational documents (Articles, Operating Agreement)
- Ownership & background documentation (for all 20%+ owners)
- Technical/security plan (encryption, data protection)
- Anti-money laundering (AML) program
- Responsible gambling policy
- Age verification procedures
- Geolocation technology description
- Marketing materials / Terms of Service
- Financial projections (3 years)
- Third-party vendor contracts (payment processors, hosting, software)

#### 4) Background Investigations
- NGCB conducts FBI/state background checks on all owners, officers, directors
- Cost included in application fee
- Timeline: 60–120 days
- You'll be contacted for additional information as needed

#### 5) Gaming License Approval
- NGCB issues decision letter
- If approved: License active, begin operations
- If denied: NGCB provides reason; may appeal
- Estimated total timeline: 4–6 months from Notice to Approval

#### 6) Ongoing Compliance
- **Monthly:** Monitor for violations, compliance audit readiness
- **Quarterly:** File reports (receipts, players, compliance incidents)
- **Annually:** Renewal (repeat lighter version of application)
- **As-Needed:** Vendor change notices, policy amendments, incident reporting

## Part 3: Federal-Level Compliance

### IRS / Tax Compliance

| Area | Requirement | Deadline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| EIN Application | Obtain EIN (Form SS-4) | Upon LLC formation |
| Tax Registration | Register for federal gaming tax (Form 8404) | Upon license approval |
| Annual Tax Return | File Form 1120 (corporate) or 1040-C (partnership/sole) | April 15 following tax year |
| Gaming Taxes | Pay federal excise tax on wagers/royalties (varies by model) | Quarterly or as incurred |
| Quarterly Estimated Taxes | Pay if profit exceeds $1,000 | March 15, June 15, Sept 15, Dec 15 |

**Resources:**
- IRS Gaming Publication 529: https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p529.pdf
- Form 8404 (Gaming License Tax): https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/form-8404

### OFAC / Sanctions Compliance

- All customers and vendors must be screened against US Treasury OFAC list
- Search tool: https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/
- Annual certification of compliance required

### FinCEN / Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

| Area | Requirement | Your Action |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| AML Program | Establish written AML program; designate compliance officer | Hire dedicated compliance person or outsource |
| Customer Due Diligence (CDD) | Verify identity of all players; collect KYC data | Implement automated KYC system |
| Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR) | File SARs with FinCEN if activity looks suspicious | Annual training; maintain SAR log |
| Currency Transaction Reports (CTR) | If handling large cash, file CTR with FinCEN | Minimal for online; still apply |
| Annual Certification | Certify to FinCEN compliance | File FinCEN Form 107 by May 1 |

**Resources:**
- FinCEN Gaming Guidance: https://www.fincen.gov/news/news-releases
- FinCEN AML Deadlines: https://www.fincen.gov/reporting-requirements

### Age Verification & Know Your Customer (KYC)

**Federal Requirements (ROSCA / COPPA):**
- Verify all players are 18+ (21+ for some gambling types)
- Collect name, date of birth, SSN, address
- Use reliable third-party verification (e.g., Equifax, ID.me, Socure)
- Retain verification records for 5+ years
- Disclose age policy in Terms of Service

**State Requirements (Nevada):**
- Geolocation technology must verify player is physically in Nevada (or approved jurisdiction)
- Test geolocation quarterly for accuracy
- Maintain logs of geolocation checks

## Part 5: Responsible Gambling & Consumer Protection

### Nevada Requirement

**Responsible Gambling Policy must include:**
1. Clear age verification messaging (18+ or 21+)
2. Problem gambling resources (NCPG, state helpline)
3. Self-exclusion option (allow players to auto-ban themselves)
4. Spending limits (allow players to set daily/weekly/monthly caps)
5. Account cooling-off period (24–7 days to reconsider)
6. Disclosure of house edge / odds / RTP (Return to Player)
7. Advertising standards (no misleading claims; must include responsible gambling message)

### Best Practices (Beyond Nevada Minimum)

- Partner with National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG): https://www.ncpg.org/
- Join ESIC (European Sports Integrity Commission) for sports betting integrity
- Implement GAMSTOP (voluntary self-exclusion registry) if you want market credibility
- Display "Play Responsibly" logos on all marketing

### Responsible Gambling Messaging (Sample)

```
PLAY RESPONSIBLY

If you think you have a gambling problem, call the National Council 
on Problem Gambling (NCPG) at 1-800-522-4700.

Gambling can become a problem. If you or someone you know has a 
gambling problem, help is available:
- NCPG: https://www.ncpg.org/ (24/7 chat & resources)
- Nevada Council on Problem Gambling: 1-800-522-4700
- Gamblers Anonymous: https://www.gamblersanonymous.org/

This site uses geolocation technology to restrict play to Nevada residents 
and other approved jurisdictions.

All players must be 18+ (21+ for some games). We verify age at sign-up.
```
